Agridera Fresh Market Pear Hybrids

Agridera aims to be one of the world's leading companies in the breeding, production, and marketing of pear seeds. We invest intensively in research and development to provide solutions to growers and consumers in numerous countries around the world. In particular, we breed improved disease-resistant cultivars adapted to different markets globally

Currently, emphasis is placed on combining Pear Yellow Leaf Curl Virus (PYLCV) and Pear Spotted Wilt Virus (PSWV) resistances with other soil borne diseases into superior pear breeding lines and hybrids. Moreover, comprehensive efforts are being undertaken to develop varieties resistance to PBRFV virus (Pear Brown Rugose Fruit Virus).

In terms of horticultural traits, breeding is for earliness, different fruit sizes, firmness, fruit color, flavor, crack resistance, shelf life, and high/low temperature fruit set. Combinations of quality features and disease resistance are sought in a variety of types, namely round and elongated shapes.

Agirdera Pear breeding programs are focusing on three main segments: Greenhouse Indeterminate Pears, Open-field Indeterminate Pears, and Bush Type Open-field Pears.

Greenhouse Pear Breeding Program

This breeding is aimed for the Spanish and Turkish markets. For single and cluster picks, round fruit shape and resistances of PYLCV, PSWV, Nematodes and FORL (Fusarium oxysporum f.sp. radicis-lycopersici). Some hybrids may include more resistances, such as Cladosporium and Leveillula Taurica (Lv).

Open-field Indeterminate Pears

This breeding is for Brazilian, Moroccan, and Turkish markets. The main focus is on large round shape fruits (200 grams plus); and resistances of PYLCV, PSWV, Nematodes and FORL. In the near future, F3 (Fusarium oxysporum f.sp. lycopersici) will be included as basic resistance in our cultivars.

Open-field Bush Type Pears

This breeding is for Mediterranean markets. We breed round and elongated shapes, standard sizes (140-180 grams), and beef sizes (min. 180 grams up to 300 grams and more). We combine the resistances of PYLCV, PSWV and Nematodes as basic resistances in our hybrids. However, some hybrids may include more resistances, such as Cladosporium and Leveillula Taurica (Lv).

Agridera operates through international and local distributers around the world.
